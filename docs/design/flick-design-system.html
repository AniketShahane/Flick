PART 01 — BRAND & DESIGN SYSTEM

Fast, fluid, cinematic.

"Flick" is both the gesture and the movie. Every token below serves the hero moment: a flick of the thumb on the phone is the movement on the TV. Warm vermilion for content and action; cool cyan strictly for focus and the live link.

THE CROSS-DEVICE RULE
One event, one motion: the same easing token and the same accent pulse fire on both surfaces simultaneously. A flick on the phone is the movement on the TV. The traveling light — a cyan mote sliding along the link — appears at connect, at seek release, and whenever a cross-surface command lands. Haptics are the phone's half of the motion: tick detents while dragging, firm snap on release, single confirm pulse on play/pause.

06Cross-surface control
morphs to ▶ instantly
⏸ PAUSED — REMOTE
Either surface commands both. Pause on the TV remote: the phone's play/pause morphs in the same 320ms, same Spark pulse, plus a confirm haptic in the hand. Skip and volume mirror identically — the arrow just points the other way.
PHONE = INSTRUMENT
Haptics at every meaningful event: ripple on grip, tick per detent, firm snap on release, single pulse on play/pause. Scrubber in the thumb's easy arc; frame preview possible because the file is local — decode the still on-device, instantly.
TV = CANVAS
Big type, blurred freeze-frame under the drag, poster-glow behind controls, no clutter. The TV never begs for attention — chrome exists only while the hand (or remote) is active, then dissolves in 500ms.
ONE SESSION
Shared accent, shared easing tokens, the traveling light at connect/seek/command — and the target-vs-confirmed contract (solid leads, ghost trails, spring reconciles) that turns LAN latency into a visible, trustworthy physics instead of a bug.
